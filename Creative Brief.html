Group 3: Quartaya Barnes, Sean Solomon, and Chantay Best

Creative Brief

Project Background

  • Client: Blockbuster
  • Product/Service: Video Store
  • SWOT: SWOT Analysis
  • Existing Research: According to The New York Times the world’s last Blockbuster still has several years left on it’s lease and does not plan on closing anytime soon. This store became the final location at the start of 2019 after the second to last location closed in Australia. On Blockbuster’s Twitter account they also announced the opportunity to experience a 90s themed sleepover for four guests on limited dates of Sept. 18-20
  • Date: September 17, 2020

Overview

  • Project type: Website Redesign
  • Reason for Project: To redesign the outdated website for Blockbuster by driving attention towards the one of a kind store located in Bend, Oregon. We are revitalizing the homepage to highlight the location of the last store. This will increase awareness of the business’ existence and peak fans interest in the opportunity to rent out the location.
  • Opportunities: One opportunity is to provide Blockbuster fans the chance to visit the last physical location in Bend, Oregon. Another is the chance to participate in a 90s themed sleepover for one night. One more opportunity we can take advantage of is the experience of video shopping as a family or a date and the memories that Blockbuster creates.

Drivers

  • Goals: To increase awareness of the last store location of Blockbuster by promoting profit generating traffic. We plan to breath life back into the dying trend of video rental by getting customers excited about the experience with a new online presence.
  • Top three objectives:
    1. Increase traffic of the last physical location.
    2. Direct customers to store rental service for sleepovers and events
    3. Sell Blockbuster memorabilia.
  • Project length: 3 months
  • Lenth of time until goal is achieved: 6 months.

Audience

  • Target Audience:
    • Age: 20-100 years old
    • Sex: Males and females
    • Education: High school diploma/GED
    • Technology competency: Beginner
    • Race: Any
    • Income Level: $0-$55,000
    • Other: People that are interested in an inexpensive video rental service and the physical aspect of picking a movie or TV show to enjoy at home.
  • What do they think of us? Blockbuster has represented so many childhood memories and is known for bringing families together for a night of entertainment.
  • Why should they care about our project and products? This is the last store of it’s kind, providing a bonding experience through video purchase in a family friendly environment.

Competitors

  • Direct Competitors:
    • Redbox
    • Netflix
    • Amazon Prime Video
    • Hulu
  • Indirect Competitors
    • Movie Tavern
    • AMC
    • Studio Movie Grill
  • What efforts could we duplicate from our competitors
    • Streaming service
    • Monthly memberships
    • Inexpensive movie choices
    • Party hosting
  • What makes us unique from the competition? We offer the last of our unique services that began in 1985 and a classical attraction visitors can come by and experience movies and games that bring on pure nostalgia.

Tone

  • What tone should we use to communicate to our target audience? Why? A fun, friendly, contemporary tone will be used to target our audience. We will revamp the website in a fun and friendly approach that will represent the feeling we all had when going into the store, browsing the latest movies and video games. While doing this, we will bring it to present-day design to improve the user experience when exploring the site. This will satisfy and encourage the audience to engage with the latest offerings the last Blockbuster site has to offer.
  • What adjectives describe the feeling we are trying to project or our approach?
    • Jovial
    • Euphoric
    • Wholesome
    • Intrigued

Message

  • What are we saying with this piece exactly? Not only are we open for business but we are making it easy to purchase memorabilia and visit the last location on earth.
  • Are the words already developed or do we need to develop them? We need to develop them into the website.
  • What do we want audiences to take away? Even though customers may not have access to the last location they can still interact and be a part of the Blockbuster community.

Visuals

  • Are we developing new images or picking up existing ones? We will be getting some existing images of people interacting with the Blockbuster’s merchandise on their social media platforms.
  • If we are creating them, who / what / where are we photographing or illustrating? And why? We will not be doing photography or illustrating these content since the location is in another state.
